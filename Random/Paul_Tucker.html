Dr. Paul Tucker

About Dr. Tucker

Dr. Paul Tucker is a leading scientist specializing in neutron scattering techniques at the ISIS Neutron and Muon Source located in Oxfordshire, England. His research focuses on exploring the atomic-level structure and dynamics of advanced materials, which has significant implications for energy, medicine, and technology.

What is Neutron Scattering?

Neutron scattering is a powerful technique that uses beams of neutrons to investigate the internal structure of materials. Unlike X-rays, neutrons interact primarily with atomic nuclei, allowing scientists to see light atoms like hydrogen and investigate magnetic properties. This makes neutron scattering invaluable in understanding everything from batteries and proteins to superconductors.

The ISIS Neutron and Muon Source

ISIS is the United Kingdom's world-leading center for research using neutrons and muons. It accelerates protons to high speeds and collides them with a heavy metal target, producing intense neutron beams. These beams are directed down beamlines to experimental stations where scientists like Dr. Tucker study material samples with incredible precision.

The facility supports a wide range of disciplines including physics, chemistry, biology, engineering, and environmental science.
